## Case Use

This profile establishes a solid conventional citation record for a historian. A current h-index of 9 is close to the lower end of the commonly cited range for full professors in history, and the listed works show sustained uptake across urban history, oral history, digital public history, and public humanities.

The larger dossier argument is that Google Scholar substantially undercounts Mark's influence because major scholarly contributions such as Curatescape and JOTPY are embodied in digital public-history infrastructures. These projects have citation, reuse, teaching, adoption, press, partner, and public-impact records that are only partially reflected in the conventional Google Scholar profile.

The strongest framing remains:

`Tebeau's conventional citation record is sufficient for history, and the fuller digital-project record is exceptional.`

If Curatescape and JOTPY are counted as authored scholarly infrastructures, they likely raise the profile beyond the visible h-index because both appear to meet or exceed the citation/use thresholds relevant to the current boundary. This should be tested in a separate project-scholarship impact table rather than asserted loosely.
