## Case Use

This capture shows a field of impact. JOTPY generated its own scholarly and methodological literature, appeared in broader archival and public history discussions, circulated through public and professional venues, and left a visible pedagogical and participatory record.

The promotion case should frame this directly: JOTPY is scholarship embodied in infrastructure. Its impact is not reducible to a conventional citation number. The stronger argument is scale plus method plus uptake. JOTPY built an archive, modeled rapid-response collecting, generated scholarship on metadata and silences, supported teaching and internships, and became a reference point for later work on pandemic archives, memory, oral history, and public humanities.

The direct JOTPY works are especially important because they show that the project built a scholarly team and a scholarly process. Mark's own article names the conceptual framework and method; the related team-authored pieces demonstrate that collaborators extended, tested, taught, and theorized that framework across institutions and contexts. That is part of the achievement, not a qualification of it.

The disciplinary context matters. Historians do not cite at the density common in many STEM or quantitative social-science fields, and digital projects are often cited less consistently than books and articles even when they substantially shape practice. For a digital public history project, a raw Google Scholar capture of this size is itself a significant marker of visibility and uptake.

The useful dossier claim is:

`A Google Scholar/Zotero capture identified a substantial body of JOTPY-related records. The evidence includes a strong core of direct JOTPY scholarship, a wider layer of scholarly/professional uptake, and a distinct layer of indexed pedagogical and archive-native artifacts. Together, this record demonstrates that JOTPY circulated as method, infrastructure, archive, and public scholarship.`
