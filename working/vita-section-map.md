## Important Interpretation Notes

The repeated "Research" labels around lines 407, 729, 897, 1023, 1093, 1121, 1205, 1331, 1415, 1499, 1555, 1569, and 1807 are course titles inside Credit-bearing Instruction, not top-level research categories. They should be read as evidence of graduate research supervision or course activity, not as a separate research section.

The Sponsored Projects / Grants section mixes several statuses: completed awards, funded-in-progress records, submitted-not-funded proposals, and submitted-for-review items. The master dossier must separate these cleanly. It can still preserve not-funded and in-review proposals as evidence of research development and ambition, but the funded total must be based only on awards.

The Technology and Other Scholarly Work sections are crucial to the dossier case. They are where ASU Vita appears to place major embodied digital/public scholarship: Curatescape, JOTPY, Climates of Inequality, Cleveland Historical, Salt River Stories, Cultural Gardens, and related infrastructure. These sections should not be relegated to service.
