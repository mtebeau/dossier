# International Reputation Case

Working note created 2026-06-17.

## Content

Mark Tebeau's international reputation should be presented as a substantive fact of the record, not as an aspiration. The case rests on the convergence of international publication, international institutional recognition, global project uptake, cross-national collaboration, and field-shaping digital public history infrastructure.

The central claim:

`Tebeau has an international reputation in digital public history, public history, oral history, and public-facing archival practice. His reputation rests not only on publications and invitations, but on the fact that scholars, curators, students, and cultural institutions in multiple countries have adopted, cited, taught with, and built from the methods and infrastructures he helped create.`

This is the right genre for the dossier. It should not say that Mark is "emerging" or "developing" an international reputation. The evidence supports a clearer claim: he is internationally recognized as a scholar and builder of digital public history infrastructure.

## Draft Case Language

Tebeau's reputation is international because his work has shaped how public historians and digital humanists understand and practice digital public history across national contexts. He co-edited *Handbook of Digital Public History*, a major international reference work in the field; served as the first Public Historian in Residence at the University of Luxembourg's Centre for Contemporary and Digital History; and has presented his work on pandemic archives, metadata, and digital public history in international settings.

That reputation is also visible in project practice. Curatescape, which Tebeau imagined and developed, became a framework for place-based digital public history adopted by cultural organizations, universities, and public history projects beyond its Cleveland origins. JOTPY likewise operated as an international rapid-response archival project, bringing together collaborators, students, institutions, and publics to document the COVID-19 pandemic in real time.

The significance is not merely that Tebeau traveled internationally or appeared on international programs. It is that his scholarly methods circulated internationally as tools, archives, frameworks, publications, and institutional collaborations.
