## Why This Matters

This is the local History standard against which the dossier must be written. It is especially useful because it explicitly recognizes public-facing scholarship, digital projects, community-engaged research, oral history, curatorial works, and creative projects as possible forms of historical scholarship.

This means the dossier should not frame digital/public history as an exception to ordinary scholarship. The History criteria already provide the institutional foundation for evaluating this work as historical scholarship when the dossier demonstrates rigor, quality, impact, role, and recognition.

## Promotion To Professor: Core Standard

For promotion to Professor, candidates are judged in Research, Teaching, and Service. The criteria require an overall record of excellence in research and teaching and significant professional service.

The research standard asks the candidate to demonstrate national or international recognition for scholarship in the field. The criteria explicitly support multiple pathways by which a candidate can make original and valuable contributions to the historical profession and broader community.

Recognized forms include traditional monographs and journal articles, but also public-facing scholarship, digital projects, community-engaged research, oral history, curatorial works, and creative projects.

The criteria recommend post-hoc review and AHA guidelines when work has not undergone traditional peer review. This matters for Curatescape, JOTPY, Cleveland Historical, Salt River Stories, ARCADE, oral history projects, public exhibits, and other embodied project scholarship.

## Research Pathways

The criteria list four pathways. The two most relevant to Mark's record are:

Pathway 3: a scholarly monograph plus a body of historical scholarship equivalent in scope, rigor, and impact to three peer-reviewed articles or book chapters.

Pathway 4: a body of historical scholarship equivalent in scope, rigor, and impact to a scholarly monograph plus three peer-reviewed articles or book chapters.

The dossier likely should not choose a pathway too early. The working claim should be that Mark's record combines conventional scholarly publications with a large body of digital/public historical scholarship whose scope, rigor, and impact meet or exceed the equivalent-work standard.

## National Or International Standing

The criteria identify examples of national or international standing, including:

- publications with prestigious presses and in top-tier journals;
- plenary talks, keynote addresses, or public readings;
- nationally competitive grants;
- prizes for published scholarship;
- invitations to lead initiatives, professional development opportunities, or workshops;
- editing a journal or book series;
- contributing to public initiatives associated with the candidate's research;
- invitations to review manuscripts for presses or journals;
- requests to assess tenure candidates at other institutions;
- measures of scholarly impact, including citations and book sales, while recognizing limits of citation indexes for historical scholarship;
- participation in national or international colloquia or symposia.

These categories map well to the dossier evidence already emerging: Luxembourg residency, international talks, Handbook of Digital Public History, Curatescape, JOTPY, editorial and reviewing work, grants, and public initiatives.

## Teaching

Required teaching indicators include a record of teaching and course development across program needs, levels, and modalities; at least two peer or supervisory reviews of teaching after tenure; evidence of mentoring undergraduate and/or graduate students to completion; and student evaluation patterns interpreted in context.

Additional teaching evidence can include teaching or mentoring awards, scholarship focused on pedagogy, and evidence of student success related to the candidate's teaching or mentoring.

For Mark's dossier, teaching should connect public history leadership, practicum supervision, field-based learning, student-built digital projects, Salt River Stories, public history methodology, and graduate mentoring.

## Service

For promotion to full professor, the criteria expect substantial service to the institution, profession, or local, state, national, or international community.

Significant service includes leadership or major participation in school, college, or university committees; editorial board or publisher work; national organization work; and community initiatives that benefit from the candidate's academic expertise.

For Mark's dossier, the service section should distinguish routine service from leadership and field-building service: Arizona History Convention leadership, editorial boards, professional reviewing, public history program leadership, digital humanities certificate work, and public/community initiatives.
