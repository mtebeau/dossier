# Cleveland Regional Oral History Collection / Cleveland Voices Assessment Starting Point

Working note created 2026-06-17.

## Content

The Cleveland Regional Oral History Collection / Cleveland Voices should be evaluated as major oral history infrastructure, public history scholarship, and community archive-building. It demonstrates long-term leadership in oral history collection, training, public access, regional history, and digital dissemination.

Mark created the initiative in 2002 and co-led it through Summer 2014. During that period, he was involved in collecting approximately 1,000 interviews, with the working range currently estimated as at least 944 and fewer than 1,049. The full collection now includes 1,381 interviews.

The collection's public reach is substantial. The Cleveland State EngagedScholarship readership map for the "All interviews" collection shows more than 250,350 downloads over the last two decades. This is a major impact marker for oral history and public history infrastructure.

This number should become a centerpiece in the oral history evidence packet. For oral history, more than 250,350 downloads is not a casual usage figure. It shows that the collection is not merely preserved; it is being actively used at scale over time. The downloads demonstrate public access, scholarly utility, community relevance, durability, and long-tail reach.

The Cleveland Regional Oral History Collection is the archival/repository layer. Cleveland Voices became the public-facing entry to that work, providing a more accessible interface for public audiences, mobile use, social sharing, printing, and interactive transcripts.

## Scope, Rigor, Impact

Scope: The project spans more than two decades, 1,381 interviews, numerous topical series, and a large network of students, faculty, public school teachers, researchers, institutions, and community contributors. Mark's leadership period from 2002 through Summer 2014 accounts for roughly 1,000 interviews, with the working range currently estimated as at least 944 and fewer than 1,049.

Rigor: The project required sustained oral history collection, training, supervision, metadata, preservation, digital publication, and public interface design. Its infrastructure connected university-based collection practices with community history and public access through EngagedScholarship and Cleveland Voices.

Impact: More than 250,350 downloads demonstrate significant public and scholarly use. The project also produced teaching, training, community memory, regional history documentation, and a durable archive used across Cleveland and Northeast Ohio history.
